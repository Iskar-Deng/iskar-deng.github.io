Hi! I’m Iskar Deng.

I am a master’s student in Computational Linguistics at the University of Washington, advised by Prof. Shane Steinert-Threlkeld. Previously, I received my bachelor’s degree in Computational and Applied Linguistics from Peking University.

My work combines computational linguistics and cognitive science. I study how language models learn linguistic structure, especially whether their behavior reflects human-like typological and cognitive biases. I am also interested in mechanistic interpretability and explainable AI.

Outside research, I play baseball and support the Seattle Mariners.
