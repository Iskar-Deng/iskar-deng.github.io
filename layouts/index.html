Hi! I’m Iskar Deng.

I am a master’s student in Computational Linguistics at the University of Washington, advised by Prof. Shane Steinert-Threlkeld. Previously, I received my bachelor’s degree in Computational and Applied Linguistics from Peking University.

My research lies at the intersection of computational linguistics and cognitive science. I study cognitive differences between humans and language models, especially in typological tendencies. I am also interested in mechanistic interpretability and explainable AI. See more details in Research.

Outside research, I play baseball and support the Seattle Mariners 🔱. See my baseball blogs in Dugout.
